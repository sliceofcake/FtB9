FtB9 is confusing.
Hopefully it will make more sense after reading this.
The most important thing to keep in mind about FtB9
All of your data is owned by you and is to be uploaded by you to a file hosting/sharing website.
FtB9 merely knows where to find your uploaded data (because you tell it), but has no control over it.
(This is a non-intuitive, confusing procedure for anyone coming from virtually any other internet service/website/application to-date.)
How to upload a chart for a first-time user (by example).
(1) Prepare and upload the following chart files (example common filetypes): .txt (the notes), .mp3 (the music), .jpg (the icon).
Example:
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/chart/tester-0/ftbTester.txt] (the notes)
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/chart/tester-0/ftbTester.wav] (the music)
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/chart/tester-0/favicon.png] (the icon)
(2) Create and upload a metadata file containing chart information as well as URLs to the aforementioned chart files.
Example:
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/chart/tester-0/meta.txt]
(3) Create and upload a user profile [if you don't have one already] that will be the owner of the chart data (with the power to remove/unlist it).
Example:
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/user/sliceofcake.txt]
(4) Add your chart URL to your user profile.
Example (see the part with "feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/chart/tester-0/meta.txt"):
[https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/user/sliceofcake.txt]
(5) Contact FtB9 staff to get your user profile listed in the FtB9 index ([https://feelthebeats.se/kern/sigma/db/FtB9]).
(6) Once your user profile is linked by FtB9 staff, you may add, edit, and delete your charts whenever you want by messing with your uploaded files accordingly.
